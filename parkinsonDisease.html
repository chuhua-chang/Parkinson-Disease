What is Parkinson's Disease?

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is the second most common neurodegenerative disease that is characterised by motor symptoms such as tremor, rigidity and bradykinesia. With over 10 million people affected, there is an urgent need to search for therapeutics to slow down, halt or prevent this debilitating neurological disorder (Source: Parkinson's Foundation).

Signs and Symptoms

Parkinson's Disease is clinically diagnosed by the presentation of motor symptoms, while several non-motor symptoms may be manifested years before diagnosis. Signs and symptoms of PD include (but not limited to):

  • Action Tremor
  • Bradykinesia
  • Rigidity in the limbs
  • Postural Instability
  • Cognitive Decline
  • Autonomic Dysfunction
  • REM Sleep Disorder

Source: www.apdaparkinson.org

Risk Factors

  1. Age
  2. Heredity
  3. Environmental exposures to toxins and pesticides
  4. Biological Sex

Source: photopharmics.com

Hallmarks of PD

PD involves the neurodegeneration of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra of midbrain, while remaining neurons contain aggregated alpha-synuclein-containing Lewy bodies.
